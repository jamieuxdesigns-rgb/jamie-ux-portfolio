Project Overview

I redesigned the Horizon Railways mobile ticketing experience to make purchasing, tracking, and validating tickets faster and more intuitive. The goal was to streamline the journey from browsing routes to confirming purchases while ensuring accessibility and ease-of-use for commuters of all ages.

The project began with user research to identify common commuter pain points, followed by mapping user journeys to optimize ticketing flows. I created wireframes to visualize the steps and high-fidelity mockups in Figma to demonstrate clear hierarchy, touch-friendly controls, and visual consistency with Horizon Railways branding.

Iterative testing and user feedback refined the ticketing interface, producing a clean, efficient design that supports both daily commuters and occasional riders. The final solution emphasizes clarity, speed, and reliability, making it easy to find critical information, scan tickets, and confirm journeys while reinforcing the Horizon Railways brand.

Before

The original Horizon Railways ticket design was static and difficult to read. Key details such as departure and arrival times, passenger information, seating, and QR codes were not visually prioritized. There were no accessibility indicators or intuitive scanning cues, making quick comprehension a challenge for users.

After – My 3 Designs

I created three distinct ticket concepts using color-coding and icons to improve usability: Blue, Red, and Yellow. Each ticket includes:

  • Train image for instant visual recognition
  • Seating map for quick reference
  • WiFi code and scannable QR code for the conductor
  • Passenger names, origin and destination
  • Date, departure time, and arrival time

These designs balance clarity with visual appeal, making it easier for commuters to read critical information at a glance while reinforcing brand identity through color and imagery.
