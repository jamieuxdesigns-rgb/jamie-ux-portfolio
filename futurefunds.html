Project Overview

Future Funds faced challenges with user engagement because the dashboard and navigation were confusing and overwhelming. The target audience consisted of millennial and Gen Z investors seeking fast, clear insights on their financial portfolios. Over a three-week design sprint, I led the project from research to high-fidelity prototypes in Figma, focusing on improving usability, visual clarity, and overall engagement.

Design Approach

Problem

The original Future Funds interface had unclear navigation, overloaded information, and a dashboard that made finding key financial insights difficult for users. These issues created friction and slowed users from completing essential tasks like tracking investments or reviewing portfolio performance.

Solution

A user-centered redesign introduced a clean, intuitive dashboard, prioritized key financial actions, improved content hierarchy, and applied professional, approachable visuals to create a more streamlined and accessible digital experience.

Outcome

The final prototype demonstrates faster task completion, clear visual trust signals, and a fully responsive layout that allows users to confidently review portfolios, track investments, and manage their finances across devices.

UX Process

🔍

Research

Analyzed user needs and examined the original website structure to identify pain points in navigation, information access, and key financial actions.

✏️

Wireframing

Developed low-fidelity wireframes to restructure the dashboard, organize financial data hierarchically, and highlight the most important actions for users.

🧩

Prototyping

Designed high-fidelity responsive screens in Figma featuring clear typography, professional visual styling, and intuitive flows that guide users through managing investments and reviewing financial data.

✅

Evaluation

Tested usability and task clarity to ensure the redesigned interface supports real-world financial users, making it easy to check portfolios, track progress, and make informed decisions.
