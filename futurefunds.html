FutureFunds Mobile App

Designing a clear, confidence‑building onboarding experience for first‑time investors.

Project Overview

Role: UX/UI Designer

Timeline: 4 weeks

Tools: Figma, wireframing, usability testing

FutureFunds is a conceptual mobile investing app created to help beginner investors feel confident taking their first steps into personal finance. Many existing financial platforms assume prior knowledge, which creates confusion and anxiety for new users. The goal of this project was to design a guided onboarding experience that simplifies complex financial concepts, reduces cognitive overload, and builds trust from the very first interaction.

Problem

First‑time investors often abandon financial apps during sign‑up because of unclear terminology, overwhelming choices, and a lack of reassurance about risk and next steps. This drop‑off prevents users from ever reaching the core value of the product.

Design Challenge: How might we create an onboarding experience that feels simple, supportive, and trustworthy for people with little to no investing knowledge?

Research & Insights

  • Reviewed onboarding flows from popular investing apps to identify friction points.
  • Noted heavy use of jargon and long multi‑step forms as key abandonment triggers.
  • Identified opportunity for plain language, progress indicators, and reassurance messaging.

Key Insight: Beginner investors need emotional reassurance just as much as functional clarity.

Design Process

1. Low‑Fidelity Wireframes

Sketched a simplified step‑by‑step onboarding flow focused on a single decision per screen, clear progress feedback, and supportive microcopy.

2. Visual Design

Designed calming color tones, strong visual hierarchy, and accessible typography to reduce anxiety and improve readability for new users.

3. Usability Testing

Conducted informal testing to observe confusion points, refine wording, and streamline the number of required steps.

Outcome & Impact

  • Reduced perceived complexity of investing for beginner users.
  • Created a calmer, more supportive first‑time experience.
  • Improved clarity through plain language and guided steps.

This project strengthened my ability to design for trust, accessibility, and emotional user needs—critical qualities in financial product design.

Reflection

Through this project, I learned how thoughtful onboarding can directly influence user confidence and long‑term engagement. In future iterations, I would expand testing with a broader participant group and explore personalized financial education within the app.
